Overview

Not every player who tries defense needs to spend one hundred and fifty dollars on a premium carbon fiber shaft right away. The STX Stallion 200 Defense Complete Stick exists for exactly that player — someone switching to defense for the first time, a young player just learning the position, or a parent who wants to see if their kid takes to the position before making a bigger investment.

At seventy nine dollars and ninety nine cents it is the most affordable way to get a complete legal defensive stick delivered to your door ready to play. STX is one of the most trusted brands in lacrosse and that reliability carries through to this entry level option.

Perfect for First Time Defenders

💰 The Smart First Buy

If you are trying defense for the first time there is no reason to spend one hundred and fifty dollars or more on a premium shaft. The STX Stallion 200 gives you everything you need to learn the fundamentals — proper pole length, a legal defense head, and playable mesh — at a price that makes sense before you commit to the position. Once you know defense is for you, then upgrade.

What You Get Out of the Box

The Stallion 200 Defense comes fully assembled and ready to play. The sixty inch pole length is regulation for all youth, high school, and adult leagues. The defense head is appropriately wide for ground balls and the soft mesh stringing is playable immediately without any break-in period needed.

For a first time defender this is everything you need. You can focus on learning the position — footwork, positioning, poke checking, ground balls — without worrying about gear. That is exactly what a beginner defensive stick should do.

Shaft & Checking Feel

The alloy shaft on the Stallion 200 is not as stiff as premium scandium or carbon fiber options. For a beginner this is actually fine — you are still developing your checking technique and a slightly more forgiving shaft is easier to learn with. As you develop your skills and your checks become more deliberate and precise you will start to feel the difference between entry level and premium shafts. That is the right time to upgrade.

Durability

The Stallion 200 holds up well for a beginner through a full season of regular play. The head maintains its shape and the shaft resists denting under normal use. Players who commit to defense long term will likely want to upgrade after one or two seasons — but for learning the position it absolutely gets the job done.

Frequently Asked Questions

Is the STX Stallion 200 Defense legal for youth and high school play?
Yes — the Stallion 200 Defense meets all US Lacrosse and NFHS equipment requirements for youth and high school play. The sixty inch length is regulation for all defensive players.
Does it come pre-strung and ready to play?
Yes — the Stallion 200 Defense comes fully assembled with soft mesh stringing and is ready to play right out of the box. No additional setup is needed.
When should I upgrade from the Stallion 200?
Once you commit to playing defense regularly and start feeling the limitations of the entry level shaft — typically after one or two seasons — it is time to upgrade. Look at the StringKing Composite 2 Pro or Warrior Burn Defense shaft as your next step.
Is this stick good for LSM players?
Yes — LSM players use the same sixty inch pole as defensive players. The Stallion 200 Defense works well for beginner LSMs learning to play both sides of the field.
