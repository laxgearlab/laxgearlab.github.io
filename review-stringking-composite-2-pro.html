Overview

The StringKing Composite 2 Pro represents the next generation of defensive shaft technology. By reinforcing key areas of the carbon fiber construction with smarter design and advanced manufacturing, StringKing has produced a shaft that is lighter, stiffer, and stronger than previous versions. For serious defensive players this shaft sets a new benchmark.

At $149.99 it sits at the premium end of the defensive shaft market. But for players who compete at a high level and want every possible edge on defense, the investment is justified.

Stiffness & Performance

Carbon fiber shafts are stiffer than alloy shafts by nature and the Composite 2 Pro takes that to the extreme. When you throw a poke check or a slap check the energy transfers directly from your hands through the shaft to the ball — there is almost no flex to absorb the impact. This translates to more effective checks and a more responsive feel on every play.

The stiffness also helps with ground ball situations. Scooping and clamping on ground balls feels more precise with a stiffer shaft because the feedback from the field is more direct.

Weight

The Composite 2 Pro is one of the lightest defensive shafts available. Carrying a 60-inch pole for an entire game is physically demanding and every gram matters by the fourth quarter. The ultralight construction means you stay faster and more explosive on defense even deep into a game.

Grip

The grip texture on the Composite 2 Pro is one of its standout features. It performs consistently in all weather conditions — dry, wet, or cold. In rain games where slippage on a standard shaft becomes a real issue the Composite 2 Pro maintains the same feel and control. Your hands stay locked in regardless of conditions.

Frequently Asked Questions

Does the StringKing Composite 2 Pro come with a head?
No — the Composite 2 Pro is a shaft only. You will need to purchase a defense head separately. Popular options include the STX Stallion and Maverik Brine defense heads.
Can carbon fiber shafts break?
Carbon fiber shafts are extremely durable under normal play conditions. They can crack on extreme direct impacts like being stepped on or hit against a hard surface at an extreme angle. Under normal checking and play they are very reliable.
Is the Composite 2 Pro legal for all leagues?
Yes — the StringKing Composite 2 Pro meets length and construction requirements for all sanctioned youth, high school, and college leagues in the US.
StringKing Composite 2 Pro vs Epoch Dragonfly — which is better?
Both are excellent carbon fiber defensive shafts. The Epoch Dragonfly is slightly lighter and costs $40 more. For most players the StringKing delivers 95% of the performance at a lower price — making it the better value pick.
