Frequently Asked Questions

Do all players need a chest protector?
Goalies absolutely need one — it's required and essential for safety. Field players wear shoulder pads which are much lighter. Check your league rules.
Can a field player wear a goalie chest protector?
Technically yes but not recommended. Goalie chest protectors restrict the range of motion field players need for shooting and passing.
What's the difference between a chest protector and shoulder pads?
Chest protectors have hard shells designed to stop lacrosse shots. Shoulder pads have foam padding designed to absorb stick checks. Goalies use chest protectors, field players use shoulder pads.
How should shoulder pads fit?
The shoulder caps should sit squarely on your shoulders with the sternum pad centered on your chest. Nothing should slide when you swing your arms fully.
Are shoulder pads required in youth lacrosse?
Yes — shoulder and arm protection are required at virtually all youth league levels. Check your specific league rules for coverage requirements.
