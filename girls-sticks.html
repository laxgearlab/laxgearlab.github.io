Buying Guide
Pocket Rules
Girls lacrosse has strict pocket depth rules — the ball must sit above the bottom shooting string. Always buy sticks marketed specifically for girls/women's lacrosse.
Head Shape
Women's heads are generally wider and more open than men's heads. This is by design — it helps with the different catching technique used in the women's game.
Shaft Length
Women's sticks are typically 35.5–43.25 inches total. Make sure the complete stick length is appropriate for your player's height.
Never Use Men's Sticks
Men's lacrosse sticks are illegal in girls/women's lacrosse due to deeper pocket rules. Always buy women's-specific equipment.
Frequently Asked Questions
Can a girl use a men's lacrosse stick?
No — men's sticks have deeper pockets that are illegal in girls and women's lacrosse. Always buy sticks specifically designed for the women's game.
What age is the STX Crux 100 for?
The Crux 100 is suitable for players aged 10 and up. For younger players (8–10), look at the Brine Mantra Youth.
Does pocket depth matter for beginners?
Yes — even beginners need a legal pocket. All sticks on this page come with legal factory pockets so you don't have to worry about adjustments.
How do I know if a stick is legal?
Hold the head horizontally. The ball should rest above the bottom of the shooting strings. If the ball sinks below them, the pocket is too deep and illegal.
