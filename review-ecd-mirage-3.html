Overview

The original Mirage launched in 2016 and immediately became the go-to head for serious shooters. ECD has refined it with every generation, and the Mirage 3.0 is the best version yet. Released in 2025 with a 10% increase in stiffness over the Mirage 2.0, a redesigned ball stop, and sharper rail cuts — the 3.0 takes everything that made the Mirage legendary and makes it better.

At 5.1 oz it maintains the same weight as the Mirage 2.0 despite being stiffer — a genuine engineering achievement. Note that you'll need to add your price in the verdict box above before uploading.

The Pro Connection

⭐ Used by PLL Pros

PLL pros Romar Dennis and Bryan Costabile use the ECD Mirage 3.0 as their primary head. Both are known for elite shooting ability — when the best shooters in professional lacrosse choose the same head, that is the strongest possible endorsement for a shooter's head.

10% Stiffer — What That Means

The defining upgrade in the Mirage 3.0 over the 2.0 is a 10% increase in stiffness. Stiffer sidewalls mean less flex on contact — whether that is a shot, a pass, or a check. Less flex means more consistent performance and more direct energy transfer on every shot. The thicker mid-rails and sharper rail cuts achieve this increased stiffness while maintaining the same 5.1 oz weight.

The three diagonal struts supporting the sidewalls significantly reduce flex on contact, and ECD specifically engineered them so the head snaps back into shape instantly after impact — meaning consistent performance throughout the game, not just when the head is fresh.

Tight Face Shape & Aggressive Offset

The tight face shape of the Mirage 3.0 is its most defining characteristic. A tighter channel means more ball hold during cradling and a cleaner, more powerful release on shots. For attackers who live to rip corners, the Mirage 3.0 pocket delivers exactly the release point and power you need.

The aggressive offset positions the pocket lower and more forward, which creates a natural shooting motion that generates more power without having to change your technique. Combined with the optimized bottom rail position, the Mirage 3.0 gives you the perfect balance of control and power on every shot.

Pocket Customization

The Mirage 3.0 features 20 evenly spaced sidewall holes, giving you extensive options for pocket customization. Whether you prefer a mid-high pocket for shooting or a mid-low pocket for cradling, the hole pattern accommodates virtually any stringing preference. Most players who buy the Mirage 3.0 unstrung prefer to have it custom strung to their specific throwing style.

Standard vs Graphene

The Mirage 3.0 is available in a standard version and a Graphene version. The Graphene version infuses the plastic with graphene — the world's strongest and lightest material — for increased durability without adding any weight. For players who are hard on their gear or want maximum longevity, the Graphene upgrade is worth considering.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is the difference between the Mirage 3.0 and Mirage 3.0 Graphene?
The standard Mirage 3.0 and Graphene versions have the same face shape and performance. The Graphene version infuses the plastic with graphene — a stronger and lighter material — for increased durability without adding weight. Choose the Graphene if you want maximum longevity.
Is the ECD Mirage 3.0 legal for high school and college play?
Yes — the ECD Mirage 3.0 conforms to both NCAA and NFHS rules. It is legal for all sanctioned high school and college lacrosse games.
Does the Mirage 3.0 come strung?
The Mirage 3.0 is available both strung and unstrung. ECD offers pre-strung versions using the same pocket setup as their pro players. Experienced players often prefer to string it themselves for a fully custom pocket.
Mirage 3.0 vs Mirage 2.0 — should I upgrade?
If you already have a Mirage 2.0 that is working well, you do not need to rush to upgrade. The 3.0 is meaningfully stiffer and has improved durability — if your 2.0 is worn out or you want the latest version the upgrade is worth it. If your 2.0 is still performing well hold off until it needs replacing.
