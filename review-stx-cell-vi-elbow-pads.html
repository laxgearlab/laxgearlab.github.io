How To Pick The Right Version

Think about where you take checks. Attack players constantly absorb slashing checks directly on the forearm — you need full arm guard coverage. Defenders use their arms to deliver checks, not absorb them — the elbow cap is all you need and the extra mobility is a real advantage when driving your pole. Middies fall in between — the arm pad gives you meaningful protection without slowing you down for the defensive end.

If you are unsure which version fits your style of play, the arm pad at $89.99 is the safest choice. It is the most versatile version of the three and works well across every position.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is the difference between the STX Cell VI Arm Guards and Arm Pads?
The Arm Guards provide full-length forearm coverage from wrist to elbow — maximum protection built for attack players in heavy contact. The Arm Pads cover the elbow and partial forearm — solid protection with more mobility, built for middies. Both use the same Cell VI foam platform.
Can a midfielder use the Arm Guards instead of the Arm Pads?
Yes — if you play a physical style and prioritize protection over mobility, you can go with the arm guards. Most middies prefer the arm pads because the extra length can restrict movement on the defensive end, but it comes down to personal preference.
Are the STX Cell VI Elbow Caps enough for attack players?
No. Attack players take consistent forearm checks from defenders, and the elbow caps leave your forearm completely exposed. Attack players should always go with the Arm Guards or, at a minimum, the Arm Pads.
Do the STX Cell VI elbow pads run true to size?
The Cell VI lineup runs true to size. Measure your arm at the elbow and reference STX's size chart. If you are between sizes, size up for a more comfortable fit.
