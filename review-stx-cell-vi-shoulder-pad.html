D3O and CURV — What Actually Makes These Pads Different

Most shoulder pads use standard EVA foam or hard plastic plates for protection. The Cell VI uses two technologies that most lacrosse pads do not. D3O is a military-grade impact material that stays soft and flexible during normal movement but stiffens instantly on impact to absorb and disperse force. CURV is a high-performance composite used in motorcycle and ski protective gear that delivers exceptional rigidity and impact resistance at a fraction of the weight of traditional plastics.

Together they let STX make the Cell VI significantly thinner and lighter than the Cell V while actually improving the protection rating. The result is a pad that does not feel like armor when you put it on — it moves with you, breathes, and only does its job when it needs to.

NOCSAE ND200-22 — Why It Matters

The Cell VI meets the updated 2022 NOCSAE ND200-22 standard, which specifically addresses cardiac protection — reducing the risk of commotio cordis, a serious condition caused by a sharp blow to the chest. Many leagues are now requiring this standard. Both the full pad and the liner meet it. If your league requires NOCSAE ND200-22, either version has you covered.

The Buckle System — Finally Fixed

One of the biggest complaints about the Cell V was the hooked buckling system that came undone constantly during games. STX completely redesigned this for the Cell VI. The new premium buckle locks securely and stays put through full practices and games. Small detail, massive improvement for anyone who played in the Cell V and dealt with constantly refastening their shoulder pad.

Full Pad vs Liner — The Simple Rule

Think about where you take checks. Attack players absorb slashing checks and shoulder contact regularly — the full pad's shoulder caps and bicep pads exist for a reason. Defenders initiate contact rather than absorb it. The extra bulk of shoulder caps and bicep pads actively gets in the way of driving your pole and delivering checks. The liner gives defenders the chest protection they need without any of the bulk that slows them down. Middies generally go with the full pad — but if you play a two-way style and prioritize mobility, the liner is a legitimate option.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is the difference between the STX Cell VI Shoulder Pad and the Liner?
The full shoulder pad includes adjustable shoulder caps and removable bicep pads for complete upper-body coverage — built for attack and midfield players who absorb regular checks. The liner strips those out for a low-profile fit with the same chest and cardiac protection — built for defenders and LSMs who need maximum mobility. Both versions are NOCSAE ND200-22 certified.
Does the STX Cell VI meet NOCSAE requirements?
Yes. Both the full pad and the liner meet the 2022 NOCSAE ND200-22 standard for chest and cardiac protection. This is the most current NOCSAE standard for lacrosse shoulder pads and is required by many leagues. Always confirm requirements with your specific league before purchasing.
How does the STX Cell VI compare to the Cell V?
The Cell VI is 17% thinner and 13% lighter than the Cell V while offering better protection. The buckle system has been completely redesigned — the Cell V's hooked buckle was notorious for coming undone mid-game. The Cell VI also adds D3O and CURV technology not present in the Cell V and meets the updated 2022 NOCSAE cardiac standard the Cell V does not.
Does the STX Cell VI run true to size?
Most players find the Cell VI runs slightly small. If you are between sizes or prefer a more comfortable fit, sizing up one size is recommended. Check STX's sizing chart which matches height and weight to pad size before ordering.
Can a midfielder use the Cell VI Liner instead of the full pad?
Yes — if you are a two-way midfielder who prioritizes mobility over maximum shoulder coverage the liner is a solid choice. However if you play a physical style and regularly absorb contact from defenders the full pad's shoulder caps and bicep pads provide meaningful additional protection that is worth the extra bulk.
