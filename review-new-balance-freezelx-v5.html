Overview

The New Balance FreezeLX V5 has built a loyal following among lacrosse players who want elite traction and genuine ankle support without paying Nike Huarache prices. New Balance has refined the FreezeLX line with each iteration, and the V5 is the best version yet — featuring updated QUIX Rail technology, an improved traction plate, and a mid-cut design that actually delivers meaningful ankle support.

At $139.99, it sits below the Nike Huarache 9 Elite while delivering performance that competes at the same level on most metrics. For players who prioritize ankle support or value the FreezeLX V5 is the cleat to buy.

QUIX Rail Traction

The signature feature of the FreezeLX line is the QUIX Rail — an outer rim of teeth along the TPU molded outsole that provides exceptional grip during lateral cutting movements. For lacrosse players who rely on hard cuts, quick direction changes, and explosive first steps, the QUIX Rail delivers consistent bite into both grass and turf surfaces.

One honest note — the QUIX Rail sits proud of the outsole, and some players have reported it scratching their opposite ankle during play. It is worth being aware of, especially if you tend to bring your feet close together during a stride. This is a minor complaint, but one that comes up consistently in player reviews.

Mid-Cut Ankle Support

Where the FreezeLX V5 separates itself most clearly from the Nike Huarache is ankle support. The mid-cut design, combined with an external heel counter, provides real structural support around the ankle — not just padding but actual lateral stability during cuts. For defenders, midfielders, and any player with a history of ankle issues, the mid-cut design is a meaningful advantage.

The external heel counter locks your heel firmly in place during gameplay, reducing internal foot movement and the energy lost to slippage inside the shoe.

Comfort & Break-In

One of the most consistent pieces of feedback from FreezeLX V5 owners is that these cleats require virtually no break-in period. The breathable mesh and synthetic upper construction conforms quickly to your foot and the snug bootie construction provides a supportive feel from the first time you lace them up. Players report wearing them straight out of the box to games without any discomfort.

At 12.7 ounces, they are lightweight for a mid-cut cleat, and the breathable mesh upper keeps feet cool during long practices and hot weather games.

Durability

The FreezeLX V5 holds up well through a full season of regular play. Multiple players report buying the same cleat season after season, specifically because of the durability — the QUIX Rail outsole maintains its grip and the upper resists breaking down under heavy use. For players who practice multiple times per week and play regular games, the V5 delivers reliable season-long performance.

Final Verdict

The New Balance FreezeLX V5 is the best mid-cut lacrosse cleat on the market and the top alternative to the Nike Huarache for players who need ankle support. The QUIX Rail traction is exceptional, the mid-cut design delivers real stability, and the zero break-in comfort makes these one of the most player-friendly cleats available at any price point.

At $139.99, it is priced at the same level as the Nike Huarache 9 Elite but delivers better ankle support thanks to the mid-cut design. For defenders, midfielders, and any player who has ever rolled an ankle, the FreezeLX V5 is the smarter buy.

Frequently Asked Questions

Do the New Balance FreezeLX V5 run true to size?
Yes — the FreezeLX V5 runs true to size for most players. Players with wider feet often find New Balance fits more comfortably than Nike. If you are between sizes, order your normal size first.
Are these cleats good for turf?
Yes — the TPU molded outsole with QUIX Rail technology performs well on both natural grass and artificial turf. For turf-only use, New Balance also makes a turf-specific version of the FreezeLX.
How long do the FreezeLX V5 last?
With regular use most players get a full season or more out of the FreezeLX V5 before the outsole shows significant wear. Many players report buying the same cleats for multiple consecutive seasons due to their durability.
FreezeLX V5 vs Nike Huarache — which is better?
It depends on your position and priorities. The Nike Huarache 9 Elite is better for attack players who want maximum mobility and a low-cut feel. The FreezeLX V5 is better for defenders and midfielders who want more ankle support and comparable traction.
